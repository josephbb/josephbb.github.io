---
title: Wise and Foolish Crowds
description: When do groups outperform individuals in cognitive tasks? Experiments and theory on collective wisdom across human and animal groups.
order: 2
---

Groups have the potential to outperform the cognitive capabilities of individuals, making better decisions with less information. Collective wisdom is widespread in animal societies and is a primary benefit of group living — but it is not an intrinsic property of any group. It depends on how individuals interact and share information.

A portion of my research focuses on the conditions that promote collective wisdom over folly, and how those conditions can be improved through platform design. This work ranges from theory on spatial and temporal correlations to empirical studies of polarization and interventions that reduce misinformation online.
