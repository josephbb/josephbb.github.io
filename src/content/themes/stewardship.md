---
title: Stewardship of Global Collective Behavior
description: Communication technology radically reshapes our global social network. How do we ensure these changes will lead towards a sustainable, equitable and healthy world?
order: 1
---

We have built and adopted technology that alters behavior at global scales without a theory of what will happen or a coherent strategy for reducing harm. Much of my research focuses on understanding the emergent consequences of digital communication technology and identifying strategies to minimize harm.

Trained as an ecologist, I spent the early years of my Ph.D. surrounded by researchers studying our natural world. Although they worked on systems ranging in scale from viruses to global climate, much of the work shared a common theme of understanding the impact of human activity on once-stable complex systems.

My research takes this framing and applies it to human social behavior. Along with co-authors across the natural, physical, and social sciences, we published a [paper in PNAS](https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.2025764118) arguing that our global collective behavior is in urgent need of stewardship. This project emerged from a 2017 workshop at Princeton and has since involved researchers at over a dozen institutions, including a [workshop](https://cyber.harvard.edu/story/2021-12/social-media-crisis-discipline) with Harvard’s Institute for Rebooting Social Media.
